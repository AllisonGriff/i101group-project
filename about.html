About Margaret

Early Life

Margaret Elaine Hamilton was born in Paoli, Indiana on August 17, 1936. She later moved to Michigan with her family, where she received her high school diploma from Hancock High School. She attended the University of Michigan in 1955 before transferring to Earlham College in Richmond, Indiana. Graduating from Earlham College in 1958, she earned a bachelors of science degree in mathematics and a minor in philosophy. Hamilton was originally married to James Cox Hamilton from 1958 until she divorced him in 1967 and later got remarried to Dan Lickly in 1969. Margaret Hamilton remains married to Lickly and is currently 88 years old. Hamilton was one of the first to refer to herself as a "software engineer", a role she held for almost her entire career.

Hamilton made many contributions to technology, software engineering, and software programming. She began her professional career at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), working in the meteorology department. During her time inii the meteorology department, Hamilton worked under the famous mathematician, Edward Lorenz. Lorenz is known as the "Father of the Chaos Theory" who also established the theoretical basis of weather and climate predictability, as well as the basis for the computer-aided atmospheric physics and meteorology.Under Lorenz, Hamilton was tasked with creating weather predicting software. Her software contributed to Lorenz's discovery of the Chaos Theory. While working on software at MIT, Hamilton coined the term "software engineering". The now popular term was ridiculed and mocked at the at the time of its creation.
